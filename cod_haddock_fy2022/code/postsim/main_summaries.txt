# Inputs and Background

## Fishing Year 2020 and 2021 Regulations

The same regulations were in place for FY2020 and FY2021.

Haddock:

*    15 fish, 17"
*    Open May1-Feb 28 and April 1- April 30
*    No Changes from 2019

Cod: 

*   1 fish, 21"
*   Open Sept 15-Sept 30 and April 1-April 14 for private anglers
*   Open Sept 8-Oct 7 and April 1-April 14 for the for-hire anglers

FY 2020 Regulations can be found [here](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2020/08/14/2020-17707/fisheries-of-the-northeastern-united-states-northeast-multispecies-fishery-fishing-year-2020).  Since there were no changes in FY2021, there is no federal register notice.

The rec sub-ACL for cod was 193mt in 2020.  


## Fishing Year 2022 Regulations

The recreational sub-ACL for GoM cod is <<dd_display: %8.0fc $cod_recACL>> mt for FY 2022. The recreational sub-ACL for GOM haddock is <<dd_display: %8.0fc $hadd_recACL>> mt.


## Data


1.  **Annual** length-frequencies for cod and haddock
1.  **Monthly** catch-per-trip for cod and haddock
    1.  The April 2021 catch-per-trip distribution for cod has 33.5% trips with 0 cod, 64.9% of trips with 1 cod, and **1.5% of trips catching 2 or more cod**.  This is really similar to March, 2021 and VERY different from May, 2021 .
	1.  May 2021: 39% of trips with 0 cod, 23% with 1 cod, 10.7% with 2, 7% with 3 cod, 3% with 4 cod, and there's a smooth decline typical of a reasonably sampled dataset.
	1.  March 2021: 40% of trips with 0 cod, 52.4% with 1 cod, 1.3% with 2, 2.7% with 3 cod, 3% with 10 cod. 
1.  2022 numbers at age for cod and haddock from the AGEPRO projections.
1.  Bottomtrawl survey data from 2017-2019 to form the age-length keys.
1.  Updated economic model parameters from Carr-Harris and Steinback (forthcoming in JOCE)

MRIP data uses "trimmed landings" estimates for Cod in FY 2020. This handles some outlier data.  These trimmed landings estimates affect the length-frequency distributions.

Scenarios 0-4:

* Averaging scenarios 1 and 3 produces scenario 0. 
* Scenario 1 should produce the highest mortality because it opens the entire month of April for both stocks.
* Scenario 2 should produce the lowest mortality because it closes April for both stocks.  It's not really under consideration though.
* Scenario 3 should produce higher haddock mortality and lower cod mortality.
* Scenario 4 should produce lower haddock mortality and higher cod mortality.  It's not really under consideration though.

Scenarios 5-12:

* These scenarios all treat the private and for-hire fleet the same in April.  They all open cod for the entire month of April.
* A good comparison will be to scenarios 0 (SQ), 1 (April open for both with  1 cod at 21"), and 4 (April open for cod at 21" and closed for haddock)
* Scenario 5 is similar to scenario 1, except the cod size limit is reduced to 20".  We expect a few more trips and a bit more cod mortality compared to Scenario 1.
* Scenario 7 is similar to scenario 1, except the cod possession limit is increased to 2 fish.  We expect a few more trips and a bit more cod mortality compared to Scenario 1.
* Scenario 9 is similar to scenarios 5 and 7, 2 cod at 20".  We expect more trips and more cod mortality compared to Scenarios 5 and 7.
* Scenario 11 is similar to scenarios 1 and 5, except the size limit is reduced to 19".  We expect more trips and more cod mortality compared to Scenarios 1 and 5.
* Scenario 12 is similar to scenarios 7 and 9, 2 cod at 19".  We expect more trips and more cod mortality compared to Scenarios 7 and 9.

Scenarios 14-17:

* Scenario 14 is a 1 fish slot limit at 22-28".  
* Scenario 15 is a 2 fish slot limit. 
* Scenario 16 is a 1 fish slot limit and the whole month of september is open to the private and for-hire fleets.
* Scenario 17 is a 2 fish slot limit and the whole month of september is open to the private and for-hire fleets.
* Scenarios 161 is  the same regulations as 16, but we allow for more choice occasions in Sept. It is a sensitivity analysis.



Scenarios 21-25: These open up cod from Sept 8-October 7 to private and for-hire anglers.

* In order to run these scenarios, I have to make an educated guess about the number of additional choice occasions that the private fleet will have when September is opened up.  The private fleet accounts for about 80% of the trips in September.  So, to open up the private fleet for the entire month, I should have 1.8* the total number of trips.  This is a bit of guesswork, it might be the case that the private anglers will fish a little more or a little less during the first half of September if it were open.  
* Scenario 21 opens all of april and September, at 1 cod and 21"
* Scenario 23 opens only September at 1 cod and 21".  April is closed for cod.
* Scenario 25 opens all of april and September, at 1 cod and 21"

* Scenarios 211 is  the same regulations as 21, but we allow for more choice occasions in Sept. It is a sensitivity analysis.

~~~~
<<dd_do:quietly>>
use "$codalkey", clear
destring, replace

foreach var of varlist * {
	capture confirm string variable `var'
		if !_rc{
			egen `var'2 = sieve(`var'), keep(n)
			destring `var'2, replace
			drop `var'
			rename `var'2 `var'
		}
	}
		
/* here is a comment
Convert CM to inches and then then round DOWN to nearest inch.  This is consistent with LNGCAT from the recreational catches 
 */
drop if age==0
drop if age==1
replace length=floor(length*$cm_to_inch) 

 /* THIS STEP CONVERTS THINGS FROM METRIC TO IMPERIAL
I use FLOOR to be consistent with the way that the length categories are constructed in the recreational data
 */
replace age=9 if age>=9

collapse (sum) count, by(age length)
/* this step fills in any missing age and length classes with missing values */
reshape wide count, i(age) j(length)
tsset age
tsfill, full

reshape long
reshape wide count, i(length) j(age)
tsset length
tsfill, full

reshape long
reshape wide count, i(length) j(age)



/* This step fills in any missing values with zeros and then does a separate lowess smoother for each age class */
foreach var of varlist count*{
	replace `var'=0 if `var'==.
	lowess `var' length, adjust bwidth(.5) gen(s`var') nograph
	replace s`var'=0 if s`var'<=0
}

reshape long count scount, i(length) j(age)
order age length
sort age length 


egen double tc=total(count), by(age)
gen double prob=count/tc

keep age length prob
replace prob=0 if prob<=9e-11

rename length t
rename prob length
reshape wide length, i(age) j(t)
rename age ageclass
tempfile alkey
save `alkey', replace
restore

keep if ageclass>=2

merge m:1 ageclass using `alkey'

foreach var of varlist length*{
replace `var'=`var'*NAA
}

collapse (sum) length* , by(year)
reshape long length, i(year) j(lngcat)
rename length num

xtset year lngcat

/*raw numbers */
xtline num if inlist(year, 2021 , 2022), overlay
<</dd_do>>
